Bridging Introduction

At the start of this year, Ian gave us a verse to carry with us — Romans 15:13. You may remember it, or have it as a printed card.

"May the God of hope fill you with all joy and peace as you trust in him, so that you may overflow with hope by the power of the Holy Spirit."

That's not just a lovely sentence to decorate a noticeboard. Ian prayed for it to do something in us — over time, through the whole year.

Introduction · Recalling Ian's Message
~45 seconds

Foundation. Focus. Fulfilment.

Ian unpacked this verse around three movements — Jesus as the foundation of our hope through the cross, the focus of our hope as we trust him now, and the fulfilment of our hope in what is still to come.

Introduction · Boaz
~45 seconds

Pork Chop Sunday

Some of you will remember the illustration Ian used. Boaz — the dog — and pork chops. After Messy Church, the chops come out of the fridge. From that moment on, completely and joyfully fixed on what's coming.

But before Sunday morning service — when he knows he's going to be left — he won't settle. He paces. Same dog. Completely different posture.

Which one looks more like us? If we're honest, for many of us, life feels much closer to that anxious pacing than that joyful expectation.

That pork chop expectation — that's what Ian was pointing us towards. Overflow — a desert after rain, suddenly alive.

Introduction · The Bridge
~45 seconds

Not the Why. The How.

Ian showed us why this verse matters — the deep roots of it, the big story it sits in, the shape of Christian hope set against the world's version.

What I'd like us to sit with this morning is something slightly different: not the why, but the how. Not the destination, but the movement.

What does it actually look like to be a person who overflows? Not what we need to achieve or produce — but how God does it. How filling works. What overflow feels like when it's quiet, ordinary, and unplanned.

As we hear these words again, listen not just for what they say — but for what God might be saying to you.

Opening Prayer

Lord Jesus, you are the God of hope.

As we come to your word this morning, we don't come because we have it all together — we come because you invite us as we are.

Fill us now with your joy and your peace. Not by our effort, but by the power of your Spirit.

That in the time we have together, something of you might overflow into each one of us — and through us, into the world you love.

Amen.

Opening
~1 min 30 sec

An Ordinary Cup of Tea

So that's the prayer, let's see what that looks like in real life.

I often make tea in the morning. One morning I made the usual two cups, and as I tried to open the bedroom door with my elbow, I wobbled and the tea spilled. Not a disaster — just a few drips down the side of the cup, easily cleaned up.

There was nothing wrong with the cup, and nothing wrong with the tea (Rosie might differ on my tea-making skills). It spilled simply because the cup was full.

The verse for the year is not about becoming bigger, stronger, or more impressive people of faith. It is about being people who are filled — so that something quietly and naturally spills into the world around us.
Romans 15:13 · Phrase by Phrase
~1 min 30 sec

"May the God of Hope…"

Romans 15:13 is not an instruction, not a target, and not a test to pass. It is a prayer. Paul is not telling the church what they must achieve; he is praying for what God longs to do in them.

That matters — especially if faith feels fragile, uncertain, new, or simply ordinary.

Not the God who gives hope, but the God of hope. Hope is part of who he is — steady, faithful, and unchanging — the same yesterday, today, and forever. And because of that, so are his promises to us.

~1 minute

"As You Trust in Him"

Trust is not having everything worked out — it's choosing where you look. It is orientation — intentionally turning towards Jesus with what we have, not what we wish we had.

Think of the boy with five loaves and two fish — small, ordinary, not enough. He offered what he had. Jesus did the rest.

What does turning look like? A prayer. A pause. Opening scripture. Lifting your eyes and saying, simply: I'm here. Fill me. The turning is the moment.

~1 minute

"Fill You with All Joy and Peace"

Notice that word — fill. Not earned, produced, nor maintained — fill.

Think of a cup being filled by a teapot — the cup doesn't fill itself. It simply sits beneath the spout and receives.

~45 seconds

"Fill You with All Joy and Peace"

Joy here is not surface happiness but a deep gladness rooted in the knowledge that God is present and faithful. Peace is not the absence of difficulty; it is wholeness — life held together even when circumstances remain unresolved.

Why does God give these gifts? Because waiting without joy becomes despair, and waiting without peace becomes anxiety. God fills us — not so life becomes easy, but so waiting becomes possible.
~1 minute

"So That You May Overflow with Hope"

God does not fill us simply for our own comfort; overflow is the intention. But overflow is not something we force. It is the natural result of being filled.

  • Sometimes that overflow is barely noticeable — a drip.
  • Sometimes it is unmistakable — a flood.
  • Both matter.

Hope moves outward into patience we did not plan, kindness we did not rehearse, and conversations we did not script.

~45 seconds

"By the Power of the Holy Spirit"

Paul finishes by lifting the weight from our shoulders: "by the power of the Holy Spirit."

Overflow is not personality, effort, or enthusiasm; it is the Spirit's work.

Which means we do not manufacture it — we make space for it. We turn our eyes and hearts towards Jesus and ask the Spirit to keep pouring.
~1 minute

Return to the Cup

  • An ordinary cup. Nothing special.
  • And because it is full, it spills — not because something has gone wrong, but because something has gone right.

Large or small, fine china or chipped mug, confident or uncertain, full of energy or feeling a little tired and worn — the vessel itself is of no concern.

What matters is not the size or shape of the vessel, but who is filling it.
~30 seconds

Return to the Cup

And when we ask the Spirit to keep pouring, the kingdom of God flows — sometimes as a drip, sometimes as a flood — but always outward, into the life of the world.

So the invitation today is simply this: to turn again towards Jesus, to bring what we have — not what we wish we had — and to ask the Spirit to fill us afresh.

~30 seconds

A Moment to Reflect

Before we go further — take a moment.

Where have you been aware of God filling you recently? However small. However quiet.

Sit with that for a moment.

The Sending · This Week, This Month, This Year
~45 seconds

Where Is Your Splash Zone?

So here's one simple thing to notice this week — and in the weeks and months ahead.

Where is your splash zone — where is hope spilling out of you?

Not where you have tried hard, or performed well, or managed to keep it together. But where something has simply flowed out of you — quietly, unexpectedly, without being planned — because you were full.

It will look different for each of us. And only you will know which of these is yours right now.

~30 seconds

Where Is Your Splash Zone?

The log flume. The dolphin show splash zone.

Perhaps this is the moment for you to make a big impact. You already know it — there is something in front of you that is bigger than a quiet conversation, bigger than a kind word. A decision, an opportunity, a step of faith that will soak everyone around you.

Is God calling you to that right now? If so, don't shrink back. That is not your overflow to manage. That is his.

~45 seconds

Where Is Your Splash Zone?

A spill of red wine on a white top.

Or perhaps it is more personal than that. Not a flood — but a mark. Something that has been noticed. A conversation that went further than you expected. A relationship where hope is quietly doing something neither of you fully planned.

You may feel a little exposed by it — like, did I just do that? Did that really come from me? Yes. It did. Because you were being filled, and it had to go somewhere. And the person on the other end of it will not forget it.

~1 minute

Where Is Your Splash Zone?

A splash of tea. A drop of water.

Or — and for many of us, most of the time, this is the truest answer — it is barely visible at all. A cup of tea carried down the hall, a little sloshing over the rim. Easily wiped. Nothing lingers. You almost certainly didn't notice it.

But someone else did. A moment of patience you didn't plan. A small act of noticing someone who needed to be seen. A word, quietly spoken, that landed somewhere you'll never know about.

That is overflow. That is the Spirit at work. Do not dismiss it because it felt like nothing. The cup was full. It spilled. That is exactly how this works.

~20 seconds

Where Is Your Splash Zone?

Most of us will leave today carrying a cup, not a log flume.

And that is enough.

~1 minute
It might be something you notice this week — a small moment you almost missed.  It might take a month before you see it.  It might be something God is quietly doing across the whole year, so gradual you only recognise it looking back.

The God of hope is still filling — right now, in this room. The Spirit is still pouring.

And the world around us is still waiting for hope to spill into it — from ordinary cups, carried by ordinary people, who have simply turned towards Jesus and asked him to keep filling.

And the extraordinary thing is this — God has chosen to let his hope enter the world that way.

Reflective Sending
~1 minute

So may the God of hope fill you, as you trust in him, with all joy and peace.

And may you overflow with hope, not by your effort, but by the power of the Holy Spirit.

And may we notice, in the days ahead, where the Spirit causes hope to spill even in the smallest of ways.

Filled with joy.

Steeped in peace.

Overflowing with hope.

Amen.
