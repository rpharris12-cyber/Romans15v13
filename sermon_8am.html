Romans 15:13

Opening Prayer

Lord Jesus, you are the God of hope.

As we come to your word this morning, we don't come because we have it all together — we come because you invite us as we are.

Fill us now with your joy and your peace. Not by our effort, but by the power of your Spirit.

That in the time we have together, something of you might overflow into each one of us — and through us, into the world you love.

Amen.

Opening
~1 minute

An Ordinary Cup of Tea

I often make tea in the morning. One morning I wobbled carrying two cups and the tea spilled — just a few drips down the side. Nothing wrong with the cup. Nothing wrong with the tea. It spilled simply because the cup was full.

This verse is not about becoming bigger or more impressive people of faith. It is about being people who are filled — so that something quietly and naturally spills into the world around us. And Paul tells us exactly where that filling comes from.
Romans 15:13 · Phrase by Phrase
~1 min 30 sec

“May the God of Hope…”

Romans 15:13 is not an instruction or a target. It is a prayer. Paul is not telling the church what they must achieve — he is praying for what God longs to do in them.

Not the God who gives hope, but the God of hope. Not just because of his character — but because of what he has done. The cross. The empty tomb. Every promise fulfilled in Christ, now opened to the whole world.

The God of hope in history, not just in theory.

~1 min 30 sec

“As You Trust in Him”

Trust is not having everything worked out — it's choosing where you look. Intentionally turning towards Jesus with what we have, not what we wish we had.

The turning is the moment. And it might look like prayer — even a single word: help. A moment of stillness before the next thing. One verse held through the day. Not boxes to tick — just the turned face.

It is simple, and it is personal — and it will look different for each of us. The only question is: where do you turn?
~2 minutes

“Fill You… So That You Overflow”

Notice that word — fill. Not earned, produced, nor maintained. A cup doesn't fill itself — it simply sits beneath the spout and receives.

God fills us with joy — not surface happiness, but deep gladness rooted in his faithfulness. And peace — not the absence of difficulty, but life held together when circumstances remain unresolved.

Waiting without joy becomes despair. Waiting without peace becomes anxiety. God knows what sustained trust requires — so he fills us with what we cannot generate for ourselves. Not so life becomes easy, but so waiting becomes possible.

And then — overflow. Not something we force, but the natural result of being filled. By the power of the Holy Spirit — which means we do not manufacture it. We make space for the Spirit to do what we cannot do for ourselves.

~45 seconds

Toward Whom?

Paul is writing to a church learning to live across division — people who got on each other's nerves, held fundamentally different views, and wouldn't have chosen each other's company. The overflow of hope is what makes unity possible.

That might be the case here. I'll leave it between you and God if someone came to mind.

But it doesn't stop at the church door. Family, friend, colleague, neighbour, the person who doesn't share your faith at all. The cup spills wherever you carry it.

~1 minute

Where Does It Spill?

For most of us, most of the time — it is barely visible. A cup of tea carried down the hall, a little sloshing over the rim. Easily wiped. Nothing lingers. You almost certainly didn't notice it.

But someone else did. A moment of patience you didn't plan. A word, quietly spoken, that landed somewhere you'll never know about — at home, at work, in a conversation you almost didn't have.

Large or small, fine china or chipped mug, confident or uncertain, full of energy or feeling a little tired and worn — the vessel itself is of no concern.

The cup was full. It spilled. That is the Spirit at work.

~45 seconds

Most of us will leave today carrying an ordinary cup — and that is enough.

That spill might be this week — a small moment you almost missed. It might be something God is quietly doing across the whole year, so gradual you only recognise it looking back.

The God of hope is still filling — right now, in this room. And the world is still waiting for hope to spill into it, from ordinary cups, carried by ordinary people.

This week, notice where the cup spills. That is where the Spirit is already at work — whether you noticed it or not.

Reflective Sending
~1 minute

So may the God of hope fill you, as you trust in him, with all joy and peace.

And may you overflow with hope, not by your effort, but by the power of the Holy Spirit.

And may we notice, in the days ahead, where the Spirit causes hope to spill even in the smallest of ways — especially toward the people we least expected.

Filled with joy.

Steeped in peace.

Overflowing with hope.

And the extraordinary thing is this — God has chosen to let his hope enter the world that way.

Amen.
