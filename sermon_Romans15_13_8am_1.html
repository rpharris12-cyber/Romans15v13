Romans 15:13

"May the God of hope fill you with all joy and peace as you trust in him, so that you may overflow with hope by the power of the Holy Spirit."

Not an instruction. Not a target. A prayer — for what God longs to do in us.

Opening
~1 minute

An Ordinary Cup of Tea

I often make tea in the morning. One morning I made the usual two cups, and as I tried to open the bedroom door with my elbow, I wobbled and the tea spilled. Not a disaster — just a few drips down the side of the cup, easily cleaned up.

It spilled simply because the cup was full.

This verse is not about becoming bigger or more impressive people of faith. It is about being people who are filled — so that something quietly and naturally spills into the world around us.
Romans 15:13 · Phrase by Phrase
~1 minute

"May the God of Hope…"

Not the God who gives hope, but the God of hope. Hope is part of who he is — steady, faithful, and unchanging. And because of that, so are his promises to us.

That matters — especially if faith feels fragile, uncertain, or simply ordinary right now.

~1 minute

"As You Trust in Him"

Trust is not having everything worked out — it's choosing where you look. It is orientation — intentionally turning towards Jesus with what we have, not what we wish we had.

Think of the boy with five loaves and two fish. Small, ordinary, not enough. He offered what he had. Jesus did the rest.

What does turning look like? A prayer. A pause. Opening scripture. Lifting your eyes and saying, simply: I'm here. Fill me. The turning is the moment.

~1 minute

"Fill You with All Joy and Peace"

Notice that word — fill. Not earned, produced, nor maintained — fill. The cup doesn't fill itself. It simply sits beneath the spout and receives.

Joy here is not surface happiness but a deep gladness rooted in the knowledge that God is present. Peace is not the absence of difficulty — it is wholeness, life held together even when circumstances remain unresolved.

God fills us — not so life becomes easy, but so waiting becomes possible.
~1 minute

"Overflow… by the Power of the Holy Spirit"

Overflow is not something we force. It is the natural result of being filled — into patience we did not plan, kindness we did not rehearse, conversations we did not script.

And Paul lifts the weight from our shoulders: "by the power of the Holy Spirit." We do not manufacture this — we make space for it.

We turn our eyes and hearts towards Jesus and ask the Spirit to keep pouring.
~45 seconds

Return to the Cup

Large or small, fine china or chipped mug, confident or uncertain, full of energy or feeling a little tired and worn — the vessel itself is of no concern.

What matters is not the size or shape of the vessel, but who is filling it.

So the invitation is simply this: to turn again towards Jesus, to bring what we have — not what we wish we had — and to ask the Spirit to fill us afresh.

~30 seconds

A Moment to Reflect

Before we go further — take a moment.

Where have you been aware of God filling you recently? However small. However quiet.

Sit with that for a moment.

The Sending
~45 seconds

Where Is Your Splash Zone?

Where is hope spilling out of you — not where you've tried hard, but where something has simply flowed out, quietly and unexpectedly, because you were full?

It will look different for each of us. And only you will know which of these is yours right now.

~30 seconds

Where Is Your Splash Zone?

The log flume. The dolphin show splash zone.

Perhaps there is something in front of you that is bigger than a quiet conversation, bigger than a kind word — a decision, an opportunity, a step of faith that will soak everyone around you.

If so, don't shrink back. That is not your overflow to manage. That is his.

~45 seconds

Where Is Your Splash Zone?

A spill of red wine on a white top.

Or perhaps it is more personal — not a flood, but a mark. A conversation that went further than you expected. A relationship where hope is quietly doing something neither of you fully planned.

Because you were being filled, and it had to go somewhere. And the person on the other end of it will not forget it.

~1 minute

Where Is Your Splash Zone?

A splash of tea. A drop of water.

Or — and for many of us, most of the time, this is the truest answer — it is barely visible at all. A moment of patience you didn't plan. A small act of noticing someone who needed to be seen. A word, quietly spoken, that landed somewhere you'll never know about.

That is overflow. That is the Spirit at work. Do not dismiss it because it felt like nothing. The cup was full. It spilled. That is exactly how this works.

~20 seconds

Where Is Your Splash Zone?

Most of us will leave today carrying a cup, not a log flume.

And that is enough.

~1 minute
It might be something you notice this week — a small moment you almost missed. It might take a month before you see it. It might be something God is quietly doing across the whole year, so gradual you only recognise it looking back.

The God of hope is still filling — right now, in this room. The Spirit is still pouring. And the world is still waiting for hope to spill into it — from ordinary cups, carried by ordinary people, who have simply turned towards Jesus.

And the extraordinary thing is this — God has chosen to let his hope enter the world that way.

Reflective Sending
~1 minute

So may the God of hope fill you, as you trust in him, with all joy and peace.

And may you overflow with hope, not by your effort, but by the power of the Holy Spirit.

And may we notice, in the days ahead, where the Spirit causes hope to spill even in the smallest of ways.

Filled with joy.

Steeped in peace.

Overflowing with hope.

Amen.
